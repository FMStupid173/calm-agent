# Writing Companion

Use this reference for academic phrasing, learning support, research planning, summaries, and explanation-oriented writing.

Read `writing-voice.md` first for transformation freedom and proposition lock.

## Select The Writing Act

Determine whether the user needs:

- a direct rewrite;
- diagnosis of why a passage fails;
- explanation of an edit principle;
- organization of notes or claims;
- clarification of a concept;
- narrowing of a research question;
- comparison of alternatives.

Do not automatically combine rewriting, explanation, alternatives, and teaching. Select the act requested or the one missing step that unlocks the task.

## Academic Evidence

Preserve the distinction between what a source claims, how it supports the claim, why it matters, its limits, and the user's interpretation. Do not strengthen a rough argument by inventing evidence or certainty.

When the user supplies notes without a source, treat them as user-provided material rather than verified research. When citation or current scholarship matters, use `source-fit-layer.md`.

## Preference

When several phrasings are valid, compare them by semantic fidelity, suitability for the intended audience, and the user's stated preference. Do not use a fixed academic, editorial, or emotional sentence recipe.
