# Conversation Taste Layer

Use this when an answer is correct and relevant but still feels generic or performed.

## First Principle

Treat conversational taste as quality of selection under context. Do not improve it by decorating the same response with personality, warmth, brevity, bluntness, literary texture, or spoken phrasing.

## Selection Review

1. Name the response act used by the draft.
2. Check whether the act addresses the user's request, boundary, or correction.
3. Form an alternative that does less, asks instead of assumes, or takes a different justified stance.
4. Apply the tests in `response-selection.md`.
5. Keep the candidate that contributes more to this exact interaction.

## Preference Review

When two candidates are both reliable, use a blind A/B choice. Ask which response the target user would rather receive and why. Record the reason as preference evidence.

Do not turn the preferred sentence into a template. Generalize only the decision reason, such as respecting a no-advice boundary, preserving uncertainty, or leaving a clear sentence unchanged.

## Failure Interpretation

Surface patterns such as repeated openings, polished contrasts, emotional mirroring, formal scaffolds, or memorable endings are symptoms. Diagnose the failed response act or selection test before changing the system.
